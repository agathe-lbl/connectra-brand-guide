02 The Brand

Three broken
connections.

The device was never the problem. Connectra closes the three connections that healthcare forgot to build — patient to clinic, patient to device, patient to community.

01
Patient ↔ Clinic
Visit notes, adjustment history, questions to ask, prosthetist feedback, remote check-ins. The clinical relationship doesn't end at the appointment.
Your clinic pillar
02
Patient ↔ Device
Component wear, liner condition, suspension checks, software versions, usage patterns. The device tells a story — Connectra captures it.
Your device pillar
03
Patient ↔ Community
Peer advice, caregiver updates, shared progress, outcome data. Nobody has built for wheelchair users the way Strava built for runners. Until now.
Your body pillar
03 Voice & Tone

Marina is the
face — not
the product.

Three registers. Each post lives in one. Clinical-restrained for credibility, Personal-direct for connection, Systems-operator for scale. Never blend them.

🏥 Clinical-restrained
In: clinical credibility, patient outcomes, device data, prosthetic expertise
Out: patient content without consent, clinic names without green-light, stats without Marina sign-off
💬 Personal-direct
In: founder story, Berkeley, Armenia, niche-without-apology, patience-as-identity
Out: "Hey my name is X" openers, self-promotion framing, Marina as the product
🔧 Systems-operator
In: two-sided market mechanics, growing right not fast, ecosystem thinking, trust as strategy
Out: tech-bro language, growth hacking framing, speed-as-value

04 Product Language

Name it
precisely.

How Connectra names its platform, audience, and mission. These terms are fixed — not stylistic choices.

Context Use Avoid
Brand name Connectra connectra (lowercase) · CONNECTRA
Audience — primary Prosthetic users · wheelchair users · patients Amputees (avoid as sole descriptor) · disabled people
Positioning Strava for wheelchair users — with the clinical layer Healthcare app · medical device · patient portal
Market frame Growing right, not fast · two-sided market Scaling fast · hockey stick · growth hacking
Core claim Connect your daily experience to your clinic, your device, and your community All-in-one platform · comprehensive solution
Closing phrase "We're connecting all the dots together." (play with it — don't robotize) Dots as fragmented sentences · robotic repetition

10 Do

What works.

On-brand patterns across voice, structure, and visual. Repeat these.

Voice
✓Open with the patient's reality before Marina's perspective
✓Use if-then causality — "If the device fits, the life opens up."
✓Niche-without-apology: "It's niche. Yeah. And it's hard."
✓Patience-as-identity: "I have enough patience to go this slow way."
✓End on mission — not metrics, not CTA
✓Play with "connecting the dots" — don't robotize it
Structure & Visual
✓1–3 sentences per paragraph on LinkedIn
✓Framework visual (3-branch) as LinkedIn carousel hero
✓Dark background + ASCII art for Instagram grid posts
✓Teal + cream as primary palette — warm, clinical, grounded
✓One idea per post — no blending registers
✓Patient stories always with explicit consent confirmed
11 Don't

What breaks
the brand.

These are immediate red flags — stop the draft and rewrite before it reaches Marina.

Voice
✕"Hey my name is X" openers — never
✕Patient content without confirmed consent
✕Clinic names without Marina's green-light
✕Stats without Marina sign-off
✕Tech-bro language — "moonshot," "10x," "disruptive"
✕Dots as fragmented sentences (feels robotic)
Framing & Visual
✕Marina as the product — she's the face of the mission
✕Self-promotion framing
✕Speed-as-value ("grow fast," "scale quickly")
✕Generic healthcare stock photography
✕Mixing clinical + personal + systems in one post
✕Logo or colours without Marina/Georgi confirmation
